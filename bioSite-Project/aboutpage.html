Short Bio

Libbie Barnes is an associate curator of exhibits at the Museum of History and Industry with a master’s degree in museum studies and has worked in the field of museums for over seven years. She is also my beloved wife, an amazing partner who shares many hobbies. We like going camping, playing board games together, doing arts and crafts, going to the movie theater, and owning a cat named Raven who is one year old, and a dog named Trixie who is turning ten years old this year.

She is a great role model for me and those around her, with a higher education, and wonderful personalities, like cracking an occasional joke in a tense situation to alleviate stress while reminding those around her to live in the moment and not dwell on the past. Since working in museums for many years, we have visited many museums together, like the Museum of Pop Culture, the San Antonio Museum of Art, the Houston Museum of Natural Science, JAMNOLA, and many more. We are also yearly pass holders at our local zoo, which is a museum, and we love to visit our favorite animals each season.

The future goal that Libbie has set is to work for a design company firm for museums to help communities thrive. This aligns with her love for community engagement and passion for the environment. More hobbies that align with these views are recycling unused clothes, thrift shopping, and cooking from home. Anyone would be amazed and enlightened to meet Libbie, who does not have to love museums but wants to learn how the world of museums works and impacts the surrounding community.
